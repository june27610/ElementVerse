Our Objectives

  • To provide easy access to information about all chemical elements — including their symbols, atomic numbers, and properties.
  • To help students and learners understand the organization of the periodic table, such as groups, periods, and element categories (metals, nonmetals, metalloids, etc.).
  • To serve as a quick reference tool for students, teachers, and science enthusiasts.
  • To make chemistry learning more interactive and engaging through color coding, hover effects, or clickable elements that show details.
  • To promote curiosity and deeper exploration of how elements relate to real-world uses and discoveries.

Recently Asked Questions

Why is the periodic table called “periodic”?

It is called periodic because the properties of elements repeat at regular intervals, or periods, when arranged by increasing atomic number.

Who created the periodic table?

The periodic table was first developed by Dmitri Mendeleev in 1869. He arranged elements according to their atomic mass and left spaces for elements that had not yet been discovered.

How are elements arranged in the periodic table?

Elements are arranged in order of increasing atomic number (number of protons). They are organized in rows called periods and columns called groups based on similar chemical properties.
